First prototype of the exoskeleton on a user

A novel design and personalised control of a soft exoskeleton

Research in Professor Robert Riener’s Sensory-Motor Systems Lab for my Master's thesis, building a novel lower-limb soft exoskeleton with a pulley-based transmission, and coding in Python its associated hierarchical control (Finite State Machine, torque profile, PID), with an EMG-based Human-in-the-loop Bayesian optimisation for the torque trajectory generation.

More info coming soon!

The ambidextrous robotic hand rotating a fidget spinner from the FRANKA arm.

Design and control of an ambidextrous musculoskeletal robotic hand via Reinforcement Learning and Teleoperation

A novel robotic non-anthropomorphic hand, featuring a four degree of freedom thumb to enhance dexterity and manipulation capabilities. The hand’s mechanical structure, consisting of a unique finger configuration with rolling contact and pin joints, along with a high-friction silicone skin and a 3D-printed concave palm, enables versatile and effective object manipulation. This, in combination with reinforcement learning, demonstrates the hand’s proficiency in simulated tasks like sphere rotation and real world applications via teleoperated object handling.

3D model of the user's gait

A Wearable IMU Device to model Patient Gait for Multiple Sclerosis

In the context of monitoring gait patterns for Multiple Sclerosis (MS) patients to determine their level of fatigue and the progression of the disease, this proof-of-concept wearable device extracts step count and step period (cadence) from its user, and applies forward kinematics to the filtered IMU acceleration signals to generate a 3D model of the user.

Medical sensor to determine a mechanical property via mechanical vibration

Ottobock Research Internship developing Medical Wearable Physiological Sensors

Based on the DE102019121254A1 patent, I developed a wearable sensor which was successful at measuring a mechanical property of a human body part with the following method: a) the human body part is excited to produce a mechanical vibration, b) the vibration is detected by a detection device, c) the detected vibration is evaluated by at least one electronic data processing device.
FFTs were used to process the vibrations.

For confidentiality reasons, more information is not available. [Patent]

The device was presented at the Great Exhibition Road Festival

A Machine Vision Collision Avoidance Control Demonstrator

With the goal to educate 11-14 year-olds on the benefits of control systems for repetitive and time-critical tasks, this arcade-like device is increasingly automated in its task to avoid incoming obstacles. Its 3 modes are:
- Fully manual continuous joystick control
- Assisted discretised joystick control
- Automated Machine Vision control

Buggy car navigating three different unseen mazes by detecting flashcards

Autonomous Robot to Navigate an Unseen Maze

As part of my "Embedded C for Microcontrollers" class, the task was to code a small buggy robot car to navigate three increasingly complex unseen mazes by detecting coloured flashcards. Once reaching the final white card, the buggy had to retrace its steps to its starting position. Our buggy succesfully completed all three mazes, being the fastest to do so from the class.

CAD model of the modular mechatronic device to reduce aerodynamic drag in the inter-carriage gap

ZEPHAERO, a Start-up to improve the Aerodynamics of Freight Trains

When COVID-19 hit in March 2020, a friend and I sought to create a startup, Zephaero (“Wind” + “Aero”), to develop a novel mechatronic system capable of reducing the drag effect generated by inter-carriage gaps in freight trains. On top of using CAD and CFD softwares, this project involved investigating legal and IP setup, interviewing key market players, and establishing a business plan.
