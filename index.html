Hi there!
I am a final year Masters Student in Robotics, Systems and Control at ETH Zürich, on a year abroad from Imperial College London. I am currently pursuing my Master's thesis on the design and control of lower-limb soft exoskeletons at the Sensory-Motor Systems Lab under the supervision of Professor Robert Riener.

My areas of interest encompass enhancing human lives through wearable sensors, assistive devices, robots, and artificial intelligence.

Medical sensor to determine a mechanical property via mechanical vibration

Ottobock Research Internship developing Medical Wearable Physiological Sensors

Based on the DE102019121254A1 patent, I developed a wearable sensor which was successful at measuring a mechanical property of a human body part with the following method: a) the human body part is excited to produce a mechanical vibration, b) the vibration is detected by a detection device, c) the detected vibration is evaluated by at least one electronic data processing device.
FFTs were used to process the vibrations.

For confidentiality reasons, more information cannot be shared. [Patent]
